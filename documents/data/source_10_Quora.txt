So a real answer.

Merced gets a bad rap because:

It’s the youngest of the UCs
It’s the least competitive of the UCs
It’s not located near… well, pretty much any urban place that Californians are eager to go to
But is it a bad choice? No.

First, all of these factors mean little in the face of the education you’re going to get. CS isn’t taught or learned better as you get closer to the coast. The students at UC Merced (especially in CS) are bright, even if they don’t have the same academic track record as their peers at some other UCs. The faculty are doing what faculty at research universities are expected to do, which is supervise grad students, bring in grants, etc.

Second, if you’re a standout, you’ll get special attention (if you want it), and it’s easier to be a standout at Merced than some other UC campuses.

So, should you consider UC Merced? Absolutely. Should you go there? Well, what are you other options and how to they compare?
